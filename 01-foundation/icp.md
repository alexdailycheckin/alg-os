# ICP - Ideal Customer Profile

Template. Filled version lives at `/workspace/foundation/icp.md`.

## Template

```
# ICP - <Operator Name>

## Primary ICP

- **Vertical:** <named industry, not "B2B" or "mid-market">
- **Revenue band or company size:** <e.g. 50m to 500m GBP turnover, 200 to 1000 employees>
- **Geography:** <country level minimum, not "global">
- **Trigger event:** <what makes them buy now, not later>
- **One named pain:** <the operational pain that survives every discovery call>

## Defensibility check

- **Three real customers who match this ICP:** <names, even if anonymised internally>
- **What was the trigger for each:** <one sentence each>
- **What do they have in common that the ICP captures:** <2 sentences>

## Secondary ICPs (optional)

<If the operator serves more than one ICP, list secondaries here. Same field structure. Note dominance: which generates 70% of revenue, etc. Do not let multiplicity weaken the primary.>

## Anti-ICP

<Buyers who look like fits but are not. Often more useful than the ICP itself for sales hygiene.>
```

## Quality bar

- All 5 primary fields are populated and pass the sparring rules in `/00-intake/sparring-rules.md`.
- "Mid-market companies" fails. "UK-based regulated financial services firms with 100m+ AUM, recently fined by FCA, with no in-house compliance lead" passes.
- The defensibility check names three real customers and captures their commonality.
- The anti-ICP is not "everyone else" but a specific lookalike segment that wastes the operator's time.
