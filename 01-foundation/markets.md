# Markets

Template. Filled version lives at `/workspace/foundation/markets.md`.

## Template

```
# Markets - <Operator Name>

## Geographies

<Country level minimum. "Global" fails. "UK, Ireland, Netherlands" passes.>

- Primary: <country / region>
- Secondary: <country / region>
- Out of scope: <where the operator deliberately does not sell>

## Verticals

<Named industries, not "all industries". Three to five maximum. If the operator claims more, sparring fires.>

1. <Vertical> - <one-line on why this vertical fits>
2. <Vertical> - <one-line>
3. <Vertical> - <one-line>

## Segments inside each vertical

<For each vertical above, name the segment by company size or revenue band. Different segments behave differently.>

| Vertical | Enterprise | Mid-market | SMB |
|---|---|---|---|
| <Vertical 1> | <served / not served, with band> | <served / not served> | <served / not served> |
| <Vertical 2> | <...> | <...> | <...> |

## Channel mix

<How buyers in each segment find this operator. Inbound from artefact, partner referral, outbound, paid, events. 3 sentences max.>

## Out of scope

<Verticals or segments deliberately not pursued. Often where margins are thin, sales cycles are long, or fit is poor.>
```

## Quality bar

- Geographies are countries, not "global."
- Verticals are named, with a reason for fit.
- Segments inside each vertical are decided. "We serve all sizes" fails.
- Out-of-scope is honest, not "we serve everyone."
