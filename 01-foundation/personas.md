# Personas - The Buying Committee

Template. Filled version lives at `/workspace/foundation/personas.md`.

## Template

```
# Personas - <Operator Name>

## Decision-maker (signs)

- **Role:** <specific title, not "C-level">
- **Reports to:** <who they answer to>
- **What they own:** <budget, P&L, KPI>
- **Quarterly metric:** <what they are measured on this quarter>
- **Trigger to engage:** <the event that makes this purchase relevant>
- **First objection:** <what they raise on the first call>
- **What unblocks them:** <proof, reference, demo, audit, pilot>

## Blocker

- **Role:** <often legal, IT, security, finance, procurement>
- **What they object to:** <one sentence>
- **What unblocks them:** <process, paperwork, validation>

## Champion

- **Role:** <specific title>
- **Why this purchase is career-positive for them:** <2 sentences>
- **What they need from us to make the case internally:** <materials, ROI calculator, case studies>

## Buying committee shape

<2 to 3 sentences describing how decisions actually get made in this ICP. Bottoms-up, top-down, consensus, single-decision-maker.>

## Persona language

<Quotes or phrases real personas use. Useful for skill output. Pulled from transcripts where possible.>
```

## Quality bar

- Each role is named with a specific title, not an abstraction.
- Each role has a trigger, an objection, and what unblocks them.
- "Buying committee" alone fails. The shape is described in concrete terms.
- Persona language is real, not generated. Quotes from sales calls beat invented phrases.
