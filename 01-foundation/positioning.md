# Positioning

Template. Filled version lives at `/workspace/foundation/positioning.md`.

## Template

```
# Positioning - <Operator Name>

## Competitor set

Three closest competitors. Named. Not "the usual suspects."

1. <Competitor name> - <one-line on what they do>
2. <Competitor name> - <one-line>
3. <Competitor name> - <one-line>

## Win axis

The one measurable axis on which this operator outperforms the named competitor set. Quantified.

- **Axis:** <speed, accuracy, scope, price, integration, expertise, data, etc.>
- **Measurement:** <the metric and the unit>
- **Gap:** <by how much, vs which competitor, with what proof>

## Concession axis

The one axis on which this operator deliberately concedes to competitors. Honest positioning has a concession.

- **Axis:** <where competitors win>
- **Why we concede:** <strategic choice, market dynamic, deliberate scope cut>

## One-line positioning statement

<Single sentence. Names the win axis and the wedge. Not a slogan.>

## Why this positioning is hard to copy

<2 to 3 sentences. The structural reason this operator can hold the win axis. Data moat, team specialism, proprietary process, accumulated case studies.>
```

## Quality bar

- All three competitors are named.
- The win axis is measurable. "Quality" fails. "Time-to-first-output measured in calendar days, 4 days vs competitor average 21 days" passes.
- The concession is real. "We concede on price (we are premium)" passes. "We win on every axis" fails.
- The positioning statement is not lift-and-shift onto a competitor's website. If it could be, the bar fails.
