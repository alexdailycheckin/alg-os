# Reference playbook: Valley, 7-figure GTM run by 1 person

A reference benchmark for ALG OS operators. Valley (founder Zayd Syed Ali) runs a 7-stage funnel built around content → signal capture → AI-personalised outbound → multichannel lead capture → email outbound → affiliate → paid amplification. The whole motion is run by 1 person.

This file records the public anatomy of the motion and maps each stage to the ALG OS skill that produces the equivalent output for an operator using the framework.

## Reported numbers

- 218 demos booked in January (launch month).
- £1M+ in pipeline every month (USD reported in the source; converted directionally).
- 3.3x to 4.5x return on spend.
- CAC payback in 1.5 months.
- Team went from 25 to 14 over the period.
- Revenue grew 4.5x.
- Run by 1 person.

These are the founder's own reported numbers. Treat as directional rather than audited.

## The 7 stages

### 1. Content machine

The founder posts on LinkedIn 6 to 7 times per week. Every post is mapped to a specific funnel stage.

- Top of funnel: founder journey and building in public.
- Middle: GTM strategy and signal-based outbound.
- Bottom: social proof and live reactions from users discovering the product.

Tooling: Virio (content engine).

### 2. Signal capture

Content feeds directly into the company's own product to capture intent signal: profile viewers, post engagers, followers, website visitors, competitor engagers. Each signal is qualified by ICP fit automatically (high, medium, low). Competitors are removed and contacts are enriched.

Reported volume: of 200 people commenting on a post, around 50 are real ICP fits.

### 3. Personalised outreach

The 50 ICP-qualified signals get a personalised message via AI agents.

Reported response: 30%+ reply rate, 40+ positive replies.

### 4. Multichannel lead capture

Leads who do not book immediately enter parallel sequences:

- Beehiiv hosts the founder's personal GTM newsletter.
- Loops sends weekly product emails.
- RB2B identifies every inbound web visitor.
- Freckle.io qualifies the inbound.
- Slack notifies the team which calls to prioritise.

Reported time to convert: 3 weeks to 3 months after first touch.

### 5. Email outbound

Cold outbound runs through Instantly.ai. Positive replies route back into the company's own product with a personalised Loom video, which is the conversion point.

### 6. Affiliate

Affiliate program runs through Tolt. 20% lifetime revenue share. Already drives 6-figure revenue.

### 7. Ads

Paid only goes behind content that already resonated organically. The objective is more reach and more followers, all funnelled back into the outreach engine to compound the loop.

## What an ALG OS operator should take from this

1. **One artefact per quarter is the content machine's spine.** A signature named-entity index produces 12 to 16 LinkedIn posts per quarter at no additional research cost. The artefact is the content; the LinkedIn posts are excerpts of the same source.
2. **Signal capture is the missing connective tissue.** ALG OS produces the artefact and the distribution copy. Operators still need a way to capture profile-view, post-engager, and competitor-engager signals and qualify them by ICP fit. AIOS Command provides this layer for Implement AI; other operators using ALG OS need an equivalent system before stage 2 works.
3. **Personalisation runs through the teardown skill.** Account-specific opening lines come from the teardown's "weak spots" and "the wedge" outputs, not from a generic template.
4. **Multichannel capture means parallel sequences, not one funnel.** Beehiiv, Loops, RB2B, Freckle, Slack are 5 separate parallel layers in the Valley playbook. The operator equivalent is newsletter, product update emails, web-visitor identification, ICP qualification, and a routing rule. ALG OS produces the source content for the newsletter and the partner emails; the routing layer is operator infrastructure.
5. **Affiliate is a long-tail compounding play.** Not in scope for ALG OS v1 but worth noting on the roadmap. The artefact's compounding mechanism (citation, trade-body, repeat-edition authority) achieves a similar long-tail revenue shape through different rails.
6. **Ads come last, behind content that already resonated.** The artefact spec's "Paid amplification" line should always read "light at edition 1, scaled at edition 3 once SEO ranks compound". Paid-first violates the loop.
